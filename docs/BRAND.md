## Why 0PENRX works

### The zero is the statement
- **Zero** middlemen.
- **Zero** markups hidden in rebate games.
- **Zero-cost generics** as the goal.
- Visually distinct — impossible to confuse with a generic brand.
- Looks intentional, not a domain fallback.

### Reads two ways simultaneously
- **0PEN → Open** — transparency, access.
- **0 → Zero** — cost, friction, gatekeepers.

### Typographically strong
- All-caps holds up as a wordmark with no additional design work.
- The `0` creates natural visual tension — the eye catches it immediately.
- Works in monospace (developer docs), serif (premium), or sans (consumer app).

---

## Positioning

**Against:** PBMs (Express Scripts, CVS Caremark, OptumRx), opaque GPO contracts,
manufacturer-to-insurer rebate games that never reach the patient.

**For:** transparent pricing, manufacturer-direct relationships, pharmacist
empowerment, patient price certainty.

**Tone:** calm authority. Not angry populist, not crypto-hype. Think *Consumer
Reports* meets *Stripe* — trusted infrastructure with clear explanations.

## Usage notes

- **Wordmark:** `0PENRX` (all caps, leading zero — never `OPENRX` or `OpenRx`).
- **Prose / domain:** `0penRX` and `0penrx.org` are acceptable in running text.
- **Tagline** appears in the header lockup and footer; keep it verbatim:
  "Zero middlemen. Open prices. Your Rx."
- **Primary color:** teal `#017a72` (light) / `#3db8ad` (dark).
- **Copyright line:** `© 2026 QANTMAI`.
