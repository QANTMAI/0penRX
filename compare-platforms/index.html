Amazon vs GoodRx vs Walmart

Three cash-pay platforms compared · Prices verified 2026-07-03 · Walmart verified 2026-07-06

Three cash-pay ways to cut prescription costs: Amazon Pharmacy + One Medical, the GoodRx card + Companion, and Walmart’s $4 generics program (the walk-in, no-account lane). 0penRX is independent and takes no commission. Figures are the vendors’ own prices (see Sources) — confirm before you sign up.

The three platforms at a glance

Amazon Pharmacy plus One Medical, the GoodRx card plus GoodRx Companion, and Walmart’s $4 generics program compared
Amazon Pharmacy + One Medical GoodRx card + GoodRx Companion Walmart $4 Prescriptions
Monthly / annual cost One Medical $9/mo or $99/yr + RxPass $5/mo — both require Prime ($139/yr or $14.99/mo). Free card $0. Companion $14.99/mo (7-day free trial). $0 — no membership. Optional Walmart+ ($98/yr or $12.95/mo) adds free delivery, not lower prices.
Prerequisites Amazon Prime — required for the $9/$99 One Medical rate and for RxPass. None. The free card needs no signup at all. None. Walk in with a prescription (first fill in person).
Prescription coverage RxPass: 50+ common generics, flat $5/mo (no brand, insulin, or controlled drugs). Prime Rx: up to 80% off generics / 40% off brand at 60,000+ pharmacies (Amazon’s stated averages: 78% / 37%). Free card: up to ~80% off brand and generic (varies by drug/pharmacy). Companion: 200+ free generics, hundreds more under $10. Gold ($9.99 / $19.99 family): up to 90% off. About 100 select generics — 30-day from $4, 90-day from $10 (tiers up to $15/$38). No antibiotics, antihistamines, steroids, or brand drugs. GoodRx-style cards also accepted (not on opioids).
Telehealth / visits One Medical: 24/7 virtual care included; in-person visits billed separately. Without membership: On-Demand Care from $29 (message) / $49 (video). Companion: $19 visits via GoodRx Care. The card and Gold include no visits. Walmart’s own clinics closed in 2024. Telehealth runs through Teladoc (Better Care Services): $89 cash per visit.
Dental / vision / lab / imaging Not bundled; labs and imaging billed by the facility. Companion adds dental, vision, lab, and imaging (MRI/CT/ultrasound) discounts. Not bundled; in-store Vision Centers billed separately.
Pharmacy network / fulfillment RxPass: mail order. Prime Rx discount: 60,000+ retail pharmacies for pickup. 70,000+ retail pharmacies — local same-day pickup. ~4,600 stores, walk-in. $10 90-day mail service; same-day delivery in 49 states (free with Walmart+; no controlled substances).
Family vs individual One Medical: +$6/mo (or $66/yr) per extra member, up to five. RxPass is per account. Companion: individual-only (no family plan). Gold family ($19.99/mo): up to 5 extra people. No plan — per-prescription pricing for anyone with a valid prescription.
Key limitations Prime required. RxPass: generic-only, no Medicaid/CHIP, not shipped to CA or WA. In-person visits cost extra. Not insurance; savings vary widely by drug and store. Companion is new (May 2026) and individual-only. List drugs only. Not in North Dakota; pricier in some states (incl. CA, MN); depends on dosage and stock; first fill in person; no opioid discounts.
Best for Prime households: mail-order generics + 24/7 virtual care, one login. Lowest local price with zero commitment (free card); all-in-one cash bundle without Amazon (Companion). Walk-in flat-price generics with zero signup; add a free discount card for drugs off the list.

Bottom line

No universal winner — pick by situation:

  • Already pay for Prime? Amazon — mail-order generics + 24/7 virtual care (RxPass skips brand and controlled drugs).
  • Want zero commitment? The free GoodRx card; add Companion ($14.99/mo) for visits and dental/vision/lab.
  • Taking common generics? Walmart’s $4 list — walk in, flat price, no card (check the ~100-drug list and your state).
