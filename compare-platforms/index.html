Amazon vs GoodRx vs Walmart

Three cash-pay platforms compared · Prices verified 2026-07-03 · Walmart 2026-07-06 · visit pricing 2026-07-07

Three cash-pay ways to cut prescription costs: Amazon Pharmacy + One Medical, the GoodRx card + Companion, and Walmart’s $4 generics program (the walk-in, no-account lane). 0penRX is independent and takes no commission. Figures are the vendors’ own prices (see Sources) — confirm before you sign up.

What you actually get

Services compared: online visits, in-person visits, dental, vision, labs, delivery, limitations
Amazon + One Medical GoodRx + Companion Walmart
Online doctor visits Yes — 24/7, included with membership. Yes — $19 per visit with a plan. Yes — $89 per visit, no plan needed.
In-person doctor visits Yes — at One Medical offices (billed separately). No. No.
Dental No. Discounts only — Aetna Dental Access network, typically 15–50% off per visit; you pay the dentist directly. Not insurance. No.
Eye exams & vision No. Discounts only — up to 30% off exams and glasses, 25% off contacts. Yes — in-store Vision Centers with in-person eye exams (price set locally; no published national price).
Labs & blood work Yes — drop-in draws at every One Medical office, run and billed separately by Labcorp/Quest. Discounts only — average savings on lab tests and imaging (e.g. MRIs, CT scans). No — pharmacy offers COVID/strep/flu test-and-treat only.
Prescription delivery Yes — RxPass and Amazon Pharmacy deliver by mail. No — the card works at local pharmacies for pickup. Yes — same-day in 49 states (free with Walmart+), plus a $10 90-day mail service.
Key limitations Prime required for best prices. RxPass: generic-only, no Medicaid/CHIP, not shipped to CA or WA. Not insurance; savings vary by drug and store. Companion is individual-only and new (May 2026). List drugs only; not in North Dakota; pricier in some states (incl. CA, MN); first fill in person; no opioid discounts.

Bottom line

No universal winner — pick by situation:

  • Already pay for Prime? Amazon — mail-order generics + 24/7 virtual care (RxPass skips brand and controlled drugs).
  • Want zero commitment? The free GoodRx card; add Companion ($14.99/mo) for $19-per-visit online care and dental/vision/lab discounts.
  • Taking common generics? Walmart’s $4 list — walk in, flat price, no card (check the ~100-drug list and your state).
