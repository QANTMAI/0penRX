Amazon vs GoodRx vs Walmart

Three cash-pay platforms compared · Prices verified 2026-07-03 · Walmart verified 2026-07-06

Three cash-pay ways to cut prescription costs: Amazon Pharmacy + One Medical vs the GoodRx card + Companion vs Walmart’s $4 generics program — the walk-in, no-account lane. 0penRX is independent and takes no commission; the figures are vendors’ own published prices (see Sources) and change often — confirm before you sign up.

The three platforms at a glance

Amazon Pharmacy plus One Medical, the GoodRx card plus GoodRx Companion, and Walmart’s $4 generics program compared
Amazon Pharmacy + One Medical GoodRx card + GoodRx Companion Walmart $4 Prescriptions
Monthly / annual cost One Medical: $9/mo or $99/yr (Prime price). RxPass drug plan: $5/mo. Both require Prime ($139/yr or $14.99/mo). All-in with RxPass: roughly $13/mo + Prime. GoodRx free card: $0. GoodRx Companion: $14.99/mo (after a 7-day free trial). No membership needed to use the free card. $0 — the $4 list needs no membership or account. Optional Walmart+ ($98/yr or $12.95/mo) adds free same-day pharmacy delivery, not lower drug prices.
Prerequisites Requires an Amazon Prime membership for the $9/$99 One Medical price and for RxPass. Without Prime, One Medical lists a higher standalone rate. Standalone. No Prime, no Amazon account, no other subscription. The free card needs no signup at all. None. No account, membership, or insurance — walk in with a prescription (the first fill must be in person).
Prescription coverage RxPass: 50+ common generic medications for the flat $5/mo (generic formulary; brand, insulin and controlled substances are outside it). Amazon Pharmacy Prime Rx: up to 80% off generics and 40% off brand when paying without insurance, at 60,000+ pharmacies (Amazon’s own disclaimer notes average savings nearer 78% generic / 37% brand). GoodRx free card: up to ~80% off, works on brand and generic (price varies by drug/pharmacy). Companion: 200+ generics free and hundreds more under $10. GoodRx Gold ($9.99/mo indiv, $19.99/mo family) offers up to 90% off on thousands of medications. About 100 select generics at flat cash prices — 30-day from $4, 90-day from $10 (some tiers $9/$24 or $15/$38). Antibiotics, antihistamines, steroids, and brand-name drugs are excluded. GoodRx-style discounts are also accepted at the counter (not on opioids).
Telehealth / visits One Medical membership includes 24/7 on-demand virtual care; in-person and scheduled visits are billed separately to you or your insurance. Without a membership, Amazon On-Demand Care is pay-per-visit from $29 (message) / $49 (video). Companion includes telehealth visits at $19 each (on GoodRx Care). The GoodRx card and Gold do not include visits — they discount the prescription only. None of Walmart’s own — its health centers and virtual care closed in 2024. Telehealth now runs through Teladoc on Walmart’s Better Care Services at $89 cash per visit.
Dental / vision / lab / imaging Not bundled. Amazon Pharmacy is a pharmacy; One Medical is primary care. Labs/imaging ordered during care are billed by the lab/facility. Companion adds discounts on dental (cleanings, exams, X-rays, crowns), vision, lab and imaging (MRI/CT/ultrasound) services. GoodRx separately markets standalone lab-test pricing. Not bundled. In-store Vision Centers remain open (billed separately); no dental or lab discounts.
Pharmacy network / fulfillment RxPass is Amazon mail-order (home delivery). The Prime Rx discount is usable at 60,000+ participating retail pharmacies for pickup. Discount card is accepted at 70,000+ retail pharmacies nationwide — you pick up locally, same-day. About 4,600 Walmart pharmacies, walk-in. A $10 90-day mail service covers program drugs; same-day delivery reaches 49 states (free with Walmart+, delivery fee otherwise; controlled substances excluded).
Family vs individual One Medical covers additional household members at +$6/mo (or $66/yr) each, up to five. RxPass is per Prime account. The free card is individual-focused; Companion is individual-only (adults only, no family plan). GoodRx Gold has a family tier at $19.99/mo covering up to 5 additional people. No plan at all — per-prescription pricing for anyone with a valid prescription; nothing to enroll in or add members to.
Key limitations Prime is a hard prerequisite for the best prices. RxPass is generic-only, not available to Medicaid/CHIP enrollees, and not sent to every state (California and Washington are excluded on the current RxPass page). In-person One Medical visits cost extra. The free card is not insurance and savings vary a lot by drug and store. Companion is new (launched May 27, 2026) and individual-only (no family plan); telehealth runs through GoodRx Care. List drugs only (about 100 generics); not available in North Dakota; prices higher in some states, including California and Minnesota; availability depends on dosage and in-stock manufacturer; first fill in person; no discount cards on opioids.
Best for People who already pay for Prime and want cheap generics by mail plus 24/7 virtual primary care under one login. People who want the lowest local counter price with zero commitment (free card), or an all-in-one cash bundle with visits and extra discounts (Companion) without buying into the Amazon ecosystem. People who want walk-in staple generics at flat prices with zero signup — the no-ecosystem lane; pair it with a free discount card for drugs off the list.

Bottom line

If you already pay for Prime, the Amazon side is hard to beat for cheap mail-order generics plus 24/7 virtual care — but RxPass skips brand and controlled drugs, and in-person visits cost extra. Want no subscription and the lowest local price today? The free GoodRx card wins on simplicity; Companion bundles visits and dental/vision/lab into one $14.99/mo plan. And if your drugs are common generics, Walmart’s $4 list may beat both with zero signup — walk in, pay the flat price, no card needed (check state availability and the ~100-drug list first). No universal winner: mail-order + ecosystem (Amazon) vs local pickup + no commitment (GoodRx) vs flat walk-in prices (Walmart).
