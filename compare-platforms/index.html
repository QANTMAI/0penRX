Amazon vs GoodRx, head-to-head
Two cash-pay bundles compared · Prices verified 2026-07-03

This page compares two popular ways for an uninsured or cash-paying patient to get both prescriptions and primary/telehealth care from one company: Amazon Pharmacy paired with One Medical versus the GoodRx discount card paired with GoodRx Companion. 0penRX is independent and takes no commission from either — the figures below are the vendors’ own published prices, each linked in Sources, and pricing changes often, so confirm before you sign up.

The two bundles at a glance
Amazon Pharmacy + One Medical GoodRx card + GoodRx Companion
Monthly / annual cost One Medical: $9/mo or $99/yr (Prime price). RxPass drug plan: $5/mo. Both require Prime ($139/yr or $14.99/mo). All-in with RxPass: roughly $13/mo + Prime. GoodRx free card: $0. GoodRx Companion: $14.99/mo (after a 7-day free trial). No membership needed to use the free card.
Prerequisites Requires an Amazon Prime membership for the $9/$99 One Medical price and for RxPass. Without Prime, One Medical lists a higher standalone rate. Standalone. No Prime, no Amazon account, no other subscription. The free card needs no signup at all.
Prescription coverage RxPass: 50+ common generic medications for the flat $5/mo (generic formulary; brand, insulin and controlled substances are outside it). Amazon Pharmacy Prime Rx: up to 80% off generics and 40% off brand when paying without insurance, at 60,000+ pharmacies (Amazon’s own disclaimer notes average savings nearer 78% generic / 37% brand). GoodRx free card: up to ~80% off, works on brand and generic (price varies by drug/pharmacy). Companion: 200+ generics free and hundreds more under $10. GoodRx Gold ($9.99/mo indiv, $19.99/mo family) offers up to 90% off on thousands of medications.
Telehealth / visits One Medical membership includes 24/7 on-demand virtual care; in-person and scheduled visits are billed separately to you or your insurance. Without a membership, Amazon On-Demand Care is pay-per-visit from $29 (message) / $49 (video). Companion includes telehealth visits at $19 each (on GoodRx Care). The GoodRx card and Gold do not include visits — they discount the prescription only.
Dental / vision / lab / imaging Not bundled. Amazon Pharmacy is a pharmacy; One Medical is primary care. Labs/imaging ordered during care are billed by the lab/facility. Companion adds discounts on dental (cleanings, exams, X-rays, crowns), vision, lab and imaging (MRI/CT/ultrasound) services. GoodRx separately markets standalone lab-test pricing.
Pharmacy network / fulfillment RxPass is Amazon mail-order (home delivery). The Prime Rx discount is usable at 60,000+ participating retail pharmacies for pickup. Discount card is accepted at 70,000+ retail pharmacies nationwide — you pick up locally, same-day.
Family vs individual One Medical covers additional household members at +$6/mo (or $66/yr) each, up to five. RxPass is per Prime account. The free card is individual-focused; Companion is individual-only (adults only, no family plan). GoodRx Gold has a family tier at $19.99/mo covering up to 5 additional people.
Key limitations Prime is a hard prerequisite for the best prices. RxPass is generic-only, not available to Medicaid/CHIP enrollees, and not sent to every state (California and Washington are excluded on the current RxPass page). In-person One Medical visits cost extra. The free card is not insurance and savings vary a lot by drug and store. Companion is new (launched May 27, 2026) and individual-only (no family plan); telehealth runs through GoodRx Care.
Best for People who already pay for Prime and want cheap generics by mail plus 24/7 virtual primary care under one login. People who want the lowest local counter price with zero commitment (free card), or an all-in-one cash bundle with visits and extra discounts (Companion) without buying into the Amazon ecosystem.
Bottom line

If you already have Amazon Prime, the Amazon side is hard to beat for cheap generic prescriptions by mail plus always-on virtual care — but you are paying for Prime whether you like it or not, RxPass skips brand-name and controlled drugs, and in-person visits are extra. If you want no subscription and the lowest price at your neighborhood pharmacy today, the free GoodRx card wins on simplicity; GoodRx Companion aims to bundle visits and dental/vision/lab discounts into one $14.99/mo plan for people who want an all-in-one option without joining Amazon. There is no universal winner — it depends on whether you value mail-order + ecosystem (Amazon) or local pickup + no-commitment flexibility (GoodRx).
