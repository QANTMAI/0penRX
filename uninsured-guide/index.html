Healthcare platforms for the uninsured
A cash-pay guide · Prices verified 2026-07-03

If you have no insurance, you are not stuck with the sticker price. A handful of platforms now sell prescriptions, telehealth, and primary care directly for cash — often at a fraction of retail. This guide lays them out side by side so you can pick, or combine, what fits. 0penRX is independent and takes no commission; every figure below is the vendor’s own published price, linked in Sources, and prices change often — confirm before you sign up. Best stacks / combinations

No single platform covers everything. These combinations tend to work well depending on your situation:

  • All-in-one: GoodRx Companion alone — one $14.99/mo plan (7-day free trial) bundling 200+ free generics plus discounts, $19 telehealth, and dental/vision/lab/imaging savings, without an Amazon account.
  • No-subscription: Cost Plus Drugs for maintenance generics by mail + Sesame for the occasional cash doctor visit (from ~$37) + an FQHC sliding-fee clinic for in-person and preventive care. Nothing recurring to cancel.
  • Amazon ecosystem: If you already pay for Prime — RxPass ($5/mo) for flat-fee common generics + One Medical ($9/mo) for 24/7 virtual primary care, all under one login.
  • Heavy prescriber: Someone filling several drugs a month — GoodRx Gold ($9.99 individual / $19.99 family) for the deepest per-drug discounts, and price-check each fill against Cost Plus and the free GoodRx card, since the cheapest source differs by drug.

Reminder: none of these are health insurance, and 0penRX is not affiliated with any of them. Figures marked “reported” (Cost Plus catalog size, Walmart’s $4/$10 list, and the Hims/Hers/Ro ranges) come from secondary sources or vendor pages that block automated fetching — we could not verify them against a live vendor page on 2026-07-03, so treat them as starting points, not 0penRX-certified facts, and confirm on the vendor’s own page.
