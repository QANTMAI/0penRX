Healthcare platforms for the uninsured

A cash-pay guide · Prices verified 2026-07-03

No insurance doesn’t mean sticker price. These platforms sell prescriptions, telehealth, and primary care for cash — often a fraction of retail. Pick one or combine. 0penRX is independent and takes no commission; figures are vendors’ own prices (see Sources) and change often. For a two-way deep dive, see the Amazon vs GoodRx head-to-head.

Start here — pick your situation

No single platform covers everything. The strongest verified combination for each situation:

  • All-in-one: GoodRx Companion alone — $14.99/mo (7-day trial): 200+ free generics, $19 telehealth, and dental/vision/lab/imaging, no Amazon account.
  • No-subscription: Cost Plus for mail-order generics + Sesame for cash visits (from ~$37) + an FQHC sliding-fee clinic for in-person care. Nothing recurring to cancel.
  • Amazon ecosystem: Already pay for Prime? RxPass ($5/mo) for flat-fee generics + One Medical ($9/mo) for 24/7 virtual care, one login.
  • Heavy prescriber: GoodRx Gold ($9.99 individual / $19.99 family) for the deepest discounts; price-check each fill against Cost Plus and the free GoodRx card, since the cheapest source varies by drug.

The details behind each pick are below, grouped by what you actually need: medications · doctor visits · both bundled.
